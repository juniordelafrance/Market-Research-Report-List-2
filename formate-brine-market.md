<p><p>Formate brine market types include sodium formate, potassium formate, and cesium formate. Sodium formate is commonly used in drilling fluids for its ability to inhibit clay swelling and improve shale stability. Potassium formate is preferred for high-density brines in completion and workover operations due to its compatibility with sensitive formations. Cesium formate is used in high-pressure/high-temperature environments for its unique properties, such as its ability to maintain a stable density over a wide range of conditions. Each formate type offers specific advantages for different applications within the oil and gas industry.</p></p>
