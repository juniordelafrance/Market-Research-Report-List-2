<p><p>Gas lasers are classified into three main types based on their operating mode: pulse gas lasers, continuous gas lasers, and other gas lasers. Pulse gas lasers emit energy in the form of short pulses, making them suitable for applications requiring high peak power. Continuous gas lasers, on the other hand, emit a continuous beam of light, making them ideal for applications that require a steady output. Other gas lasers include excimer lasers, chemical lasers, and metal vapor lasers, each with their own unique characteristics and applications within the market.</p></p>
