Drum brakes utilize brake shoes that press outwards against a rotating drum to slow down the vehicle. On the other hand, disc brakes use brake pads that clamp down on a rotating disc to stop the vehicle. Both types of brake systems use hydraulic pressure to amplify braking force, providing enhanced braking performance and safety for vehicles.</p></p>
