<p><p>Bag-in-box containers are commonly made using materials such as Low Density Polyethylene (LDPE), Ethylene Vinyl Acetate (EVA), and Ethylene Vinyl Alcohol (EVOH). LDPE is preferred for its flexibility and durability, while EVA provides good barrier properties against oxygen and moisture. EVOH is another popular choice due to its excellent barrier properties against oxygen transmission. Other materials used in bag-in-box containers include polypropylene and aluminum foil, each offering unique advantages such as enhanced barrier properties and puncture resistance.</p></p>
