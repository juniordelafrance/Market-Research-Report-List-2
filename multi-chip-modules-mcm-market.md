<p><p>Multi-Chip Modules (MCM) are semiconductor devices where multiple integrated circuits (ICs) are packaged together in a single module. The rising demand for high-performance computing (HPC) solutions and the proliferation of Internet of Things (IoT) devices are also driving the growth of the MCM market.</p><p>Some of the latest trends in the Multi-Chip Modules (MCM) market include the development of advanced MCM platforms with enhanced thermal management capabilities, increased integration of heterogeneous components on a single module, and the emergence of wafer-level packaging techniques for MCMs. Additionally, the adoption of 3D stacking technology and the shift towards system-in-package (SiP) solutions are expected to further drive the market growth in the coming years.</p></p>

<p><p>Multi-Chip Modules (MCM) come in different market types - MCM-L, MCM-D, and MCM-C. MCM-L, or Laminate MCMs, consist of multiple chips mounted on a single substrate. MCM-D, or Discrete MCMs, have individual components mounted together in a small package. MCM-C, or Ceramic MCMs, use ceramic substrates for better thermal performance. Each type caters to different requirements and applications, offering varying levels of integration, performance, and cost-effectiveness in the semiconductor industry.</p></p>
