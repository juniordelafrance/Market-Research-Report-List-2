<p><p>Battery Electric Vehicles (BEVs) are fully electric vehicles that run entirely on electricity stored in a battery. They do not have a gasoline engine and produce zero emissions. On the other hand, Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEVs) have both an electric motor with a battery to provide electric power, as well as a gasoline engine for longer trips. PHEVs can run on electricity alone for a limited range before switching to the gasoline engine, offering the flexibility of both fuel sources.</p></p>
