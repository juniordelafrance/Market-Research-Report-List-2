<p><p>Interference microscopes are commonly categorized into two main types: double beam microscopes and multibeam microscopes. Double beam microscopes utilize two separate beams of light to create interference patterns, allowing for high-resolution imaging and precise measurements. On the other hand, multibeam microscopes use multiple beams of light to achieve even higher resolution and sensitivity. Both types of interference microscopes are widely used in various fields such as nanotechnology, materials science, and biological research for their ability to provide detailed images and measurements of microscopic structures.</p></p>
